中国高铁有多赚钱

2018-09-20 分类：美国 信息来源：三泰虎

Godfree Roberts, Ed.D. Education & Geopolitics, University of Massachusetts, Amherst (1973)

Updated Sep 6, 2017 · Upvoted by Xinchun Li, lives in China

Yes, China’s high speed rail is profitable and there aren’t many Chinese who haven’t personally profited from it, either. It’s an excellent, highly visible example of how China designs its programs holistically– whether they’re for infrastructure, social security or defense–and eliminates what Western economists call ‘externalities’ (things, like pollution, that someone else must pay for. There is no ‘someone else’ in China; there’s only ‘us’).

是的，中国高铁是可以盈利的，而且大部分中国人都从高铁中获益。这是一个极好的、非常明显的例子，说明了中国是如何整体设计其项目的——无论是基础设施、社会保障还是国防——并消除了西方经济学家所称的“外部效应”(如污染等必须由“别人”买单的东西)。在中国没有“别人”，只有“我们”)。

One of the most famous, most popular high speed rail lines on earth is the 400 km London-Paris Eurostar: “The company awarded a contract for the construction of the tunnel to TransManche Link (TML). The tunnel cost around £9.5bn to build, about double TML's original estimate of £4.7bn. The tunnel was financed partly from investment by shareholders and partly from £8bn of debt, and was officially opened on 6 May 1994 by HM Queen Elizabeth II, and President François Mitterrand. In its first year of operation the company lost £925m because of disappointing revenue from passengers and freight, together with heavy interest charges on its £8bn of debt. In April 2004, a dissident shareholder group led by Nicolas Miguet succeeded in taking control of the board. However, in February 2005, Jean-Louis Raymond, the Chief Executive appointed after the boardroom coup, resigned and Jacques Gounon took complete control becoming Chairman and Chief Executive. In July 2006, shareholders voted on a deal which would have seen half the debt, by then reduced to £6.2bn, exchanged for 87% of the equity. However this plan failed, and on 2 August 2006, the company was placed into bankruptcy protection by a French court for six months”.

全球最著名、最受欢迎的高铁线路之一是长达400公里的伦敦至巴黎的“欧洲之星”。隧道由联营体（TML）承建，建造成本约为95亿英镑，是TML最初估价47亿英镑的2倍左右，部分投资来自股东，80亿英镑来自债务，并于1994年5月6日正式对外开放,由英国女王伊丽莎白二世和弗朗索瓦•密特朗总统剪彩。第一年运营，公司亏损了9.25亿英镑，因为旅客和货运收入令人失望,加上80亿英镑债务的沉重利息。2004年4月，以尼古拉斯•米盖为首的持不同意见的股东团体成功控制了董事会。然而，在2005年2月，在董事会政变后被任命的首席执行官让•路易•雷蒙德辞职，雅克•高农完全控制了公司，成为了董事长和首席执行官。2006年7月，股东们投票通过了一项交易,将债务减少到62亿英镑，换取87%的股权。然而，这个计划失败了，在2006年8月2日，该公司被法国法院申请破产保护6个月。

Eurostar’s experience confirmed what neoliberal critics had long claimed: infrastructure is unprofitable even if it benefits millions of people. So it’s natural that, when the Chinese government built 20,000 km of even higher-speed rail, skeptics predicted bankruptcy. High-speed rail service in China was introduced on April 18, 2007 and traffic has grown 30% per annum ever since, reaching 1.44 billion in 2016: four times of the HSR volume in Japan, nine times France’s, and greater than north America’s total air traffic.

欧洲之星的经历证实了新自由主义批评人士长期以来的观点：基础设施是无利可图的，即使它惠及了数百万人。因此，当中国修建了2万公里高铁时，持怀疑态度的人自然而然预测会破产。2007年4月18日，中国开通了高铁服务，自那以后，运输量每年以30%的速度增长，在2016年达到14.4亿人次：是日本高铁总量的四倍，法国的九倍，超过北美的空中交通总量。

Despite these impressive figures, every completed line incurs losses in its first years of operation. The Beijing–Tianjin HSR cost ¥20.42 billion to build and ¥1.8 billion annually to operate, (including ¥0.6 billion in interest payments) and needed to provide 40 million rides a year to reach profitabilty. In its first year, 2008–2009, it carried 18.7 million riders and generated ¥1.1 billion in revenues for a loss of ¥0.7 billion. The next year, ridership rose to 22.3 million and revenues improved to ¥1.4 billion, for a ¥0.5 billion loss. By 2016 it was maxed out, providing 100 million rides–100% capacity–and highly profitable. Construction of a second line has commenced. (WikiVisually).

尽管这些数字令人印象深刻，但每条线路在运营的头几年都会出现亏损。京津高铁建设成本204.2亿人民币,运营费用每年18亿人民币(包括6亿的利息)，每年需要提供4000万次乘车服务才能实现盈利。在第一年（2008 - 2009）,它运送了1870万名乘客和营收为11亿人民币，亏损7亿人民币。第二年,客流量升至2230万和收入提高至14亿，亏损5亿。到2016年，它已经达到了顶峰，运送了1亿人次，利润丰厚。第二条线路的建设已经开始。(WikiVisually)。

China’s HSR network has exceeded its predictions–both technically and financially–and is solidly profitable today.

中国的高铁网络已经超出了它的预测——无论是在技术上还是在财务上——而且在今天仍然有可观的利润。

Where do the profits come from and how are profits diverted to pay off construction loans? The answers are: they come from almost everywhere and, since every aspect of the network is cooperatively owned, it’s easy to skim off enough pay the interest and retire the bonds when they fall due. ‘Everywhere’ is a little vague, so here’s a list of the cost savings and some revenue streams:

利润从何而来，如何将利润用于偿还建筑贷款?答案是：几乎来自所有地方，而且由于网络的每个方面都是合作所有的，所以很容易就能获得足够的利息，并在债券到期时将其赎回。“所有地方”这个词有点模糊，因此这里列出了成本节约和收入来源:

ŸIncreased forecasting accuracy. The more they have built, the more accurate their predictions have become. Accurate predictions lead to accurate financial models which lead to accurate bond pricing.

预测准确性的提升。他们建造得越多，预测就越准确。准确的预测带来准确的金融模型，从而促成准确的债券定价。

ŸLow construction cost. The program benefits from scale. From mass-produced, unballasted rail sections to bridges (ordered by the meter) to engines and carriages. Production and demand are tightly integrated, eliminating stop-and-go and costly downtime. Current construction and manufacturing costs are now 30%-40% cheaper than anywhere on earth. Here’s an example of how automated the process is: the SLJ900/32 Bridge Girder Erector Machine lays a pre-fabbed tranche of track in 50 minutes, much faster than if done by 20 workers. High-Speed Railways in China: A Look at Construction Costs

低建设成本。该项目得益于规模，从大批量生产的、未压舱铁路到桥梁，再到发动机和车厢。生产和需求紧密结合，消除了走走停停和昂贵的停工期。目前的建筑和制造成本比世界上任何地方都低30%-40%。这里有一个例子来说明这个过程的自动化程度：SLJ900/32桥梁架设机在50分钟内铺设了一段预制轨道，比20名工人完成的速度快得多。

ŸEconomic stimulus from the initial investment. A surprising percentage of the $650 billion invested (so far) came back within twelve months in the form of income and sales taxes that ripple out in all directions from the factories and construction sites. HSR construction creates jobs and lifts demand for construction, steel and cement industries during the economic downturn. Work on the Beijing–Shanghai HSR mobilized 110,000 workers.

最初投资的经济刺激。到目前为止，6500亿美元投资的一部分在12个月内以收入税和销售税的形式收回来了。在经济低迷时期，高铁建设创造了就业机会，提高了对建筑、钢铁和水泥行业的需求。京沪高铁的建设雇用了11万名工人。

ŸLand development. This is the system’s ace card: Those shopping complexes at the stations? A share of the rents goes to pay off the bonds. Land sales around the stations? A share of the sales price goes to pay off the bonds. The increased commercial activity around stations? A share of the increased taxes help pay off the bonds. Those ‘stations in the middle of nowhere’? When new towns are built around them, land sales and development and the increased tax base will help pay off the bonds.

土地开发，这是张王牌。车站的购物中心--一部分租金用来偿还债券。车站周围的土地出售--一部分销售收入用来偿还债券。车站周边的商业活动增加--增加的税收有助于偿还债券。那些“地处偏僻位置的车站”--当在他们周围建造新城镇时，土地销售和开发以及增加的税基将有助于偿还债券。

ŸIncreased productivity from passenger time savings. Improves economic productivity and competitiveness over the long term by increasing the transport capacity of railways and linking labor markets. The World Bank’s survey found that high-speed passengers' average income is 35-50% higher than that of passengers on conventional trains. This year (2017), if 1.8 billion riders each saves an average of four hours compared to automobile, plane or regular train and the average urban wage in 2017 is US $1,000/mo, then HSR will save the average rider $24 of productive time, for a total of $43 billion. that will, eventually, boost economic activity and, of course, tax revenues.

节省乘客时间而增加的生产力。通过提高铁运输能力和连接劳动力市场，提高经济生产率和竞争力。世界银行的调查发现，高铁乘客的平均收入比普通列车高出35-50%。今年(2017年)，相比比汽车、飞机或普通火车，如果18亿乘客平均每人节省4小时，2017年城市平均工资是1000美元/月，那么高铁将为每位乘客节省24美元的生产时间，总计430亿美元。这最终将促进经济活动，当然还有税收。

ŸTicket revenues. Since the first line opened, ticket prices have stayed almost flat while the average wage has doubled. Today, twice as many people can afford HSR as could afford it in 2007.

车票收入。自从第一条线路开通以来，票价基本持平，而平均工资翻了一番。如今，负担得起高铁票价的人是2007年的两倍。

ŸEnhanced freight revenues. Intercity high speed package delivery is a large and growing revenue source. Today someone who lives in Shanghai can order products from Alibaba in the morning and get them in the evening thanks to the HSR network, another reason why China's e-commerce is flourishing. Improved productivity and competitiveness generate increased taxes. Moving passengers to high-speed lines frees up older railways to carry more freight, which is more profitable for railways than passengers.

货运收入的增加。城际高速包裹递送是一个巨大且不断增长的收入来源。如今，住在上海的人早上从阿里巴巴订购产品，晚上就能收到货，这要归功于高铁网络，这也是中国电商蓬勃发展的一个原因。生产率和竞争力的提高带来了税收的增加。把乘客转移到高铁线路上，可以让老旧的铁路运输更多的货物，相比乘客，这对铁路系统来说更有利可图。

ŸAdvertising revenues. Billions of ‘impressions’ to passengers whose average income is 30–50% higher than the national average.

广告收入。对平均收入比全国平均水平高出30-50%的乘客，留下数以十亿计的“印象”。

ŸConcession revenues (food and drink). Those snack carts, station concessions, drink dispensers? They’re paying off the bonds, too.

特许权收入(食物和饮料)。那些小食车，车站特许权，饮料机等等，它们也在偿还债券。

ŸEnvironmental benefits. HSR reduces noise, high-atmosphere pollution and CO2 dramatically. These are direct contributions to quality of life and indirect contributions to reducing atmospheric pollution, thus raising the value of surrounding land.

环境效益。高铁能显著降低噪音、大气污染和二氧化碳。这些是对生活质量的直接贡献，对减少大气污染的间接贡献，从而提高了周围土地的价值。

ŸTourism revenues. ‘The results indicate that at the national level, a trend of convergence emerges during the three-year period of analysis in both China and Korea. At the HSR passing area level, HSR contributes to accelerate regional economic convergence and reduce the regional income disparities in China’. HSR is responsible for 59% of the increase in market potential for the secondary cities connected by bullet trains. (Economic geographers call market potential "a geographic area's access to markets for inputs and outputs"). A 10% increase in a secondary city's market potential is expected to be associated with a 4.5% increase in its average real estate price. HSR promotes the growth of second-tier cities by making them more livable/desirable–and, of course, collects taxes on the increased revenues that go to pay….you know the rest.

旅游收入。结果表明，在国家层面，中国和韩国在3年的分析期间都出现了趋同趋势。在高铁通过区域，高铁有助于加速区域经济融合，缩小中国区域收入差距。高铁对高铁连接的二线城市市场潜力的增长贡献了59%。(经济地理学家把市场潜力称为“一个地理区域的投入和产出的市场准入”)。二线城市的市场潜力增长10%，预计其平均房地产价格将增长4.5%。高铁促进二线城市的增长使他们更宜居/更受欢迎, 增加的税收收入用来支付....你懂的。

ŸNational cohesion and a national market. People, products and services move quickly, allowing people to live in one place and work in another. HSR helps rural areas profit from their natural resources by bringing business opportunities and tourists–while reducing rural people’s isolation (think Tibet!).

民族凝聚力和国家市场。人、产品和服务移动迅速，人们的居住地点和工作地点可以不同。高铁通过带来商机和游客，帮助农村地区从自然资源中获利，同时加强农村居民与外界的联系(比如xz!)

ŸIntellectual property exports. China is a leading source of high-speed rail technology. Chinese train-makers have absorbed imported technologies quickly, localized production processes and began competing in the export market. Six years after receiving Kawasaki's license to produce Shinkansen E2, CSC Sifang produced the CRH2A without Japanese input (Kawasaki ended cooperation with Sifang and has been sulking ever since).

知识产权出口。中国是高铁技术的主要来源国。中国火车制造商迅速吸收了进口技术，将生产过程本土化，并开始在出口市场展开竞争。在获得川崎制造新干线E2的许可6年后，四方CSC在没有日本人参与的情况下生产了CRH2A(川崎中断了与四方的合作，从那以后一直闷闷不乐)。

ŸEnergy savings. Electric trains use less energy to transport people and goods on a per unit basis and can draw power from more diverse sources of energy–including renewables–than automobile and aircraft, which are more reliant on imported petroleum. This cuts billions from China’s energy import bills.

节省能源。与汽车和飞机相比，电力火车运送人员和货物所消耗的能源更少，而且可以从更多样化的能源来源获得电力，包括可再生能源，而汽车和飞机更依赖进口石油。这将为中国减少数十亿美元的能源进口支出。

ŸScale savings: more profitable lines subsidize less profitable lines that serve thinly populated areas (think Tibet again).

规模节约：利润更高的线路补贴利润更少的线路，服务于人口稀少的地区(xz)。

Because the government controls all these factors for the benefit of all the people, it can skim off enough from each to easily repay the bonds.

因为政府为了全体人民的利益而控制所有这些因素，所以它可以从每个因素中捞取足够的钱来轻松偿还债券。

And this is just one of hundreds of such projects underway whose planners approach them in the same holistic way.

这只是正在进行中的数百个项目之一，这些项目的规划者也以同样的方式来处理这些项目。
